Can diabetes be cured? +

Medical Consensus (ADA 2023):
- Type 1: No cure, managed with insulin
- Type 2: Remission possible through sustained weight loss (10-15% body weight) and lifestyle changes
- Gestational: Usually resolves post-pregnancy but increases Type 2 risk by 50%

Source: Diabetes Care Journal, Vol 46

What foods help manage blood sugar? +

ADA Recommended Foods:
1. Non-starchy veggies (broccoli, spinach)
2. Whole grains (quinoa, oats)
3. Lean proteins (fish, tofu)
4. Healthy fats (avocados, nuts)
5. Low-glycemic fruits (berries, apples)

Source: ADA Nutrition Guidelines

What A1C level indicates diabetes? +

Diagnosis Criteria (WHO):
- Normal: Below 5.7%
- Prediabetes: 5.7% to 6.4%
- Diabetes: 6.5% or higher
‚Äª Test every 3-6 months if diagnosed

Source: World Health Organization

Can stress cause diabetes? +

Mechanism:
- Chronic stress ‚Üí Increased cortisol ‚Üí Insulin resistance
- 68% higher Type 2 risk in high-stress jobs (Johns Hopkins Study)
- Stress management reduces HbA1c by 0.5% on average

Source: Diabetes UK Research

Is gestational diabetes permanent? +

Postpartum Guidance:
- Usually resolves after delivery
- 50% develop Type 2 within 10 years
- Required: Annual glucose test + Mediterranean diet

Source: CDC Diabetes Pregnancy Guidelines
